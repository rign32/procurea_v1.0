# RFQ Comparison Template — Download Pack

A working buyer's template for comparing 2-20 supplier quotes side-by-side, with a defensible weighted scoring model that survives Internal Audit review.

## What's in this bundle

- **`rfq-comparison-template.md`** — the main buyer's guide. Three fully worked RFQ examples (commodity / strategic / regulated), the 10 things suppliers always omit from quotes, a Notes-column micro-guide, and a worked weighted-scoring example with a weight-swap. Read this first.
- **`rfq-comparison-template.csv`** — the core comparison table with 10 fields and 3 pre-filled sample rows (machined metal parts, EUR pricing). Open in Excel or Google Sheets and extend.
- **`rfq-comparison-template.xlsx`** — Excel version with the formulas pre-built.
- **`rfq-comparison-template-notion.md`** — Notion database schema with formula snippet, views, and seed data. Use this if your team collaborates in Notion.
- **`INSTRUCTIONS-TO-CONVERT.md`** — how to turn the CSV into a proper Excel workbook with live formulas, a Weights tab, and a Summary tab. 15 minutes.
- **`cover.webp`** — cover art.

## Start here

1. Open **`rfq-comparison-template.md`** and read the three worked examples (pages 4-9). They show what a filled-in comparison looks like for commodity, strategic, and regulated categories.
2. Open **`rfq-comparison-template.xlsx`** (or import the CSV into Google Sheets).
3. Before you send the RFQ: pick your weighting scheme (commodity / strategic / regulated), version-lock it, and email the sheet to your category director. This is the audit-defence step.
4. Fill the sheet as quotes arrive. Use the Notes micro-guide in section 4 of the main MD.
5. Score once all rows are complete. Don't score incomplete rows against complete rows.

## The 10 comparison fields (recap)

Every RFQ comparison should capture at least these ten data points for each supplier. Any field left blank after the quote deadline flags the row as "incomplete" — do not score incomplete rows against complete rows.

1. **Supplier Name** — legal entity name as it appears on the quote.
2. **Country** — country of production, not country of sales office.
3. **Unit Price (EUR)** — normalize every quote to a single currency and single Incoterm before scoring.
4. **MOQ** — in units, not in €.
5. **Lead Time (days)** — from PO confirmation to FCA/FOB/DDP at the named endpoint.
6. **Payment Terms** — Net 30 / Net 45 / Net 60 / T/T 30/70 / 50% deposit.
7. **Certifications** — name them with number and expiry; "Certified" is not a certification.
8. **Incoterm** — Incoterms 2020. EXW ≠ DDP by up to 12% of unit price.
9. **Sample Available** — Yes-free / Yes-paid / No / Pending.
10. **Notes** — free text. See section 4 of the main MD for what good notes look like.
