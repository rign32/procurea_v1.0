## Copy angle (1 sentence)
A 14-location casual-dining chain discovered they were paying 14% over market on fresh produce by running a structured sourcing sweep that took 3 days instead of the 3 months a consultant had quoted.

## Stats pills (3-4)
- **From 3 to 12 vendors** in the approved pool

- **€420k** annual produce spend re-benchmarked

## Must-include elements
- Context: anonymized client — "a Central-European casual-dining chain, 14 locations, ~€6M annual food spend."
- The problem: F&B director inherited a 5-year-old vendor list. Quarterly category reviews had been dropped during COVID. Margins compressing. No time for a full procurement consultant engagement.
- The Procurea sweep: three category searches (fresh produce, meat/poultry, dairy), each returning 50-100 regional wholesalers. Filtered by HACCP status, delivery radius (80km from commissary), minimum order volume compatibility.
- Outreach + bids: 35 vendors contacted in PL + DE (border region suppliers), 23 responded, 12 were bid-ready. Price spread vs. incumbents revealed 14% average premium on incumbents.
- Implementation: winner ran a 60-day parallel test at 2 locations before full rollout. Incumbent vendors were given the benchmark and offered a re-negotiation window; 1 of 3 matched, 2 were replaced.
- Honest framing: fresh produce has quality variance beyond price; the chain kept an incumbent as backup vendor for peak-season supply. Price isn't everything.
