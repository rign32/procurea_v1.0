## Copy angle (1 sentence)
A residential developer expanded their subcontractor short list from 3 recurring partners to 15 bid-ready HVAC companies in 2 weeks, giving them real competitive tension for the first time in 4 projects.

## Stats pills (3-4)
- **15 qualified subcontractors** bid (vs. 3 typical repeat invites)

- **27%** lowest-bid price spread (new competitive tension)
- **200-unit residential project**, €2.4M HVAC package

## Must-include elements
- Context: anonymized developer — "a Polish residential developer with €80M annual turnover, running 2-3 concurrent 100-300 unit projects."
- The problem: HVAC package had been awarded to the same 3 subcontractors for 4 consecutive projects; prices creeping up; no real benchmarking.
- The Procurea pipeline: query "HVAC installation subcontractors 200+ unit residential Poland" → 80 results screened, 22 relevant, 18 enriched with current licenses and references, RFQ sent to 18.
- Response dynamics: 15 responded within 10 days, 3 declined (capacity), 3 non-responsive (chased 2x, gave up). Response rate 83%.
- The bid outcome: wide price spread revealed the incumbents had drifted 8-12% above market. Winner was a new supplier at 19% below highest bid, project references validated on site visits.
- Honest framing: the winning bidder required 2 months of supplier onboarding (safety docs, insurance, financial checks). The sourcing saved weeks but not the qualification overhead.
