## Copy angle (1 sentence)
A D2C cosmetics brand moved 3 SKUs from a Chinese private-label producer to 2 alternative EU manufacturers (Poland + Italy) in 3 weeks, cutting lead time from 90 days to 28 and earning "Made in EU" marketing claim for the Q4 holiday push.

## Stats pills (3-4)

- **3 weeks** discovery → signed production order (vs. 4-month typical nearshore cycle)
- **90 → 28 days** lead time reduction
- **"Made in EU" claim** unlocked for Q4 campaign

## Must-include elements
- Context: anonymized client — "a D2C skincare brand, ~€4M revenue, 8-person team, 12 SKUs, previously 100% private-label from 2 Chinese factories."
- The trigger: Red Sea shipping costs up 2.5x in Q1, 90-day lead times created stockouts for their hero product, and their biggest retailer asked for "Made in EU" qualification by Q4.
- The Procurea run: primary search "cosmetics private label manufacturers EU ISO 22716" → 120 results, screened to 60 relevant, enriched with certification status and MOQ info → 24 bid-eligible (MOQ < 3000 units). Multilingual outreach in IT/PL/DE/ES, 18 responses.
- Qualification: 8 producers requested samples, 4 approved, 2 selected (1 Polish for hero SKU volume, 1 Italian for premium SKU). Duration: 2 weeks sampling + validation.
- Commercial outcome: higher unit cost (+12% nominal) but fully offset by lower shipping (-40%) and lead time (-69%) — landed cost roughly neutral but working capital freed and retail shelf claim unlocked.
- Honest framing: the migration was PARTIAL, not total — China remained a backup for 2 less critical SKUs. "Insurance, not divorce" — link to China+1 post.
