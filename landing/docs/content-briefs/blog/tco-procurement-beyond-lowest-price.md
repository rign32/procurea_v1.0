## Copy angle (1 sentence)
"TCO matters" is a truism — this post ships the math: 7 hidden-cost categories, typical percentage of unit price they add, and a CFO-defensible template that turns a 3% unit-price savings into a 12% TCO loss.

## Must-include elements
- The 7 hidden cost categories: (1) freight + duties + handling, (2) quality/defect rate + rework + returns, (3) inventory carrying cost (extended lead time = more safety stock), (4) payment terms cost (Net 30 vs. Net 90 DSO impact), (5) switching costs (tooling, qualification, NDA), (6) supplier risk premium (insurance, dual sourcing redundancy), (7) administrative overhead (PO errors, invoice disputes, expediting).
- Worked example: two suppliers, Supplier A at €9.80/unit + China freight + 3% defect rate + Net 30, Supplier B at €11.20/unit + Poland freight + 0.8% defect rate + Net 60. Compute TCO. B wins despite being 14% more expensive nominal.
- The "total landed cost" model (TLC) — a subset of TCO focused on logistics, explain when to use TLC vs. full TCO.
- Category patterns: where TCO matters most (high-defect-risk components, long-lead-time items, custom tooling), where unit price alone is fine (commodity low-value high-volume).
- How to run a TCO analysis in 2 hours, not 2 weeks — shortcuts that don't lose rigor.
- Negotiation lever: showing a supplier their TCO vs. competitor's. Often unlocks price + terms concessions.
- Quote for LinkedIn: "Unit price is what Finance asks for. TCO is what your CEO remembers six months later when the line stops."
