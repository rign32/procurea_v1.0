## Copy angle (1 sentence)
The 30-hour number is industry folklore until you break it down — this post itemizes the 7 tasks inside one sourcing campaign (avg. 30h), shows where 80% of the time goes (research + follow-up), and calculates the actual hourly cost-per-campaign for a team earning €60k avg.

## Must-include elements
- The 7-task time breakdown for one campaign (avg. values from primary research or pilot data, with ranges): Define scope (2h), Research suppliers (8h), Email discovery + validation (6h), First outreach (3h), Follow-up chasing (6h), Response parsing + comparison (3h), Decision + handoff (2h).
- Cost-per-campaign calculator: 30h × loaded hourly rate (€40-€80 depending on market) = €1200-€2400 per campaign. Multiply by campaigns per year (15-30) = €18k-€72k fully-loaded.
- Margin impact math: if sourcing cycle drops from 21 days to 7 days, and that means 2 additional campaigns/year/buyer, that's €100k+ additional spend sourced at better rates per buyer. Show the chain.
- Where AI realistically saves time (research, email discovery, follow-up) vs. where it does NOT (scope definition, final decision, relationship management). Honest framing.
- Interactive element suggestion: a simple embedded calculator (3 inputs: hourly rate, campaigns/year, hours/campaign → output: annual cost).
- Quote for LinkedIn: "If your buyers spend 30 hours per campaign and you run 25 campaigns a year, you're paying €60,000 for the privilege of having them do research — not procurement."
