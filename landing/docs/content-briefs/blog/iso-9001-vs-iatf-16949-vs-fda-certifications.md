## Must-include elements
- The core 6 always-worth-checking: ISO 9001 (quality), ISO 14001 (environment), ISO 45001 (safety), IATF 16949 (automotive), ISO 13485 (medical devices), FDA registration (US medical/food/cosmetics).
- Category-specific must-haves: Automotive (IATF 16949 + VDA 6.3), Medical (ISO 13485 + MDR + FDA), Food (HACCP + BRCGS + IFS), Cosmetics (ISO 22716 + FDA registration), Electronics (ISO 9001 + RoHS + REACH + IPC), Apparel (OEKO-TEX + GOTS + BSCI).
- The "certification theater" problem: 3 common certs that are low-bar and don't predict quality (ISO 9001 alone, self-declared RoHS, uncertified supplier audits).
- Registry links for verification: IATF oscar.iaob.org, ISO-certified (varies by cert body — IAF, UKAS), FDA FURLS, OEKO-TEX label check, GOTS public DB, EU MDR EUDAMED.
- The "expired cert" problem: average certificate validity is 3 years, but 22% of suppliers keep using expired logos. How to build a re-check cadence.
- Table: Cert → Category → Registry → Re-check cadence → What it actually proves.
- FAQ: "is iso 9001 enough", "difference iatf 16949 vs iso ts 16949", "do i need supplier audits if they have iso 9001".
