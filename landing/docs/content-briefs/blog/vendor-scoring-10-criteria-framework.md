## Copy angle (1 sentence)
Vendor scoring content reuses the same 5 generic criteria (price, quality, delivery, service, technology) — this framework separates category-universal criteria from category-specific ones and gives a weighting model defensible to Internal Audit.

## Must-include elements
- The 10-criteria framework split into 3 tiers: Tier 1 universal (5 criteria — price competitiveness, quality track record, lead time reliability, financial stability, responsiveness), Tier 2 compliance (3 criteria — certifications current, audit readiness, regulatory coverage), Tier 3 strategic (2 criteria — innovation/roadmap, cultural fit/partnership potential).
- Weighting logic: why equal weights (10% each) are wrong, how to adjust per category (commodity = price-heavy, strategic component = quality + stability heavy).
- Scoring rubric per criterion: 1-5 scale with anchored descriptions (what a "3" looks like vs. a "5"). Remove subjectivity.
- Worked example: 4 suppliers scored on 10 criteria, weighted, totaled. Show how the "cheapest" supplier ranks 3rd after weighting.
- Calibration: run 2 analysts through the same suppliers, compare scores. Gap > 1.5 points means rubric needs more anchoring.
- Audit-trail: how to document why each criterion scored what it scored. The text field that matters more than the score.
- Warning: scoring theater — when the numbers look rigorous but the weights were set after the decision was made.
