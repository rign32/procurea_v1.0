## Copy angle (1 sentence)
Nearshoring content on the internet is either geopolitical hand-waving or Eastern-European-agency brochureware — this is a category-by-category operator's guide covering plastics, textiles, electronics, metals, and packaging with honest trade-offs per country.

## Must-include elements
- Country breakdowns with real strengths/weaknesses for 5-7 categories: Poland (plastics, metals, assembly), Czechia (precision machining, automotive tier 2), Portugal (textiles, footwear, cork), Turkey (textiles, white goods, chemicals), Romania (wiring harnesses, automotive), Spain (food processing, steel), Bulgaria (low-cost textiles + electronics assembly).
- A decision framework: 5 questions a procurement lead must answer before starting a nearshore pilot (category criticality, volume, lead-time tolerance, certification overlap, supplier density).
- Cost-reality table: China FOB vs. Poland FCA vs. Turkey FCA for a representative 1000-unit MOQ across 3 categories. Include total landed cost (duties, logistics, currency risk).
- Risk section: labor cost inflation (Poland wages up 12% YoY 2024), currency exposure, EU carbon border adjustment (CBAM) implications for steel/aluminum.
- Downloadable playbook CTA appears at three points: intro, mid-post, end.
- Quote-worthy line (for LinkedIn): "The question is no longer 'should we diversify from China' — it's 'which three categories move first, and what does your 2027 supplier map look like if you start this quarter.'"
