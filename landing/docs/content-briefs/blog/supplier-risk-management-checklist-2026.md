## Copy angle (1 sentence)
Most SRM content is ISO 31000 + consultant diagrams — this is an operator's 12-point checklist (financial, operational, geopolitical, regulatory, ESG) with the specific data source we use to verify each point.

## Must-include elements
- The 12-point framework organized in 5 risk categories: Financial (creditworthiness, payment history, ownership changes), Operational (single-source, capacity, quality record), Geopolitical (country risk, sanctions exposure, CBAM), Regulatory (certifications current, licenses valid, audit compliance), ESG (carbon reporting, labor audits, conflict minerals).
- For each of the 12 points: (a) what the risk looks like in practice, (b) the data source to verify (VIES, D&B, Dun credit, GSA SAM, OFAC, Handelsregister, ISO certification registries), (c) frequency of re-check.
- A risk-scoring matrix: how to weight low/medium/high scores per supplier and convert into a single 1-100 risk score. Include a worked example.
- Callout: "Signals that a supplier is about to fail" — 6 leading indicators (delayed invoice responses, key staff on LinkedIn "open to work", sudden discount offers, website not updated in 6 months, credit rating downgrade, ownership change).
- 2026 additions beyond classic SRM: CBAM readiness (EU), CSRD reporting scope, US Section 232 exposure, Red Sea shipping risk.
- Embedded checklist teaser → gated PDF magnet CTA.
- FAQ: "how often to re-verify suppliers", "supplier risk score calculation", "srm framework iso 31000 vs practical".
