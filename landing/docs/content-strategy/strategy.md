## 1. Vision & Mission

**Vision**: Make Procurea the *first place* procurement operators look when they want to understand AI-native sourcing, nearshoring, multilingual outreach, and structured offer comparison. Not the biggest library. The one where every article answers a real question a buyer actually types into Google at 9:14am on a Tuesday.

**Mission (6 months)**: Ship **20 lean, specific, keyword-anchored posts** plus amplification across newsletter and LinkedIn so that a P1/P2/P3 landing on procurea.io either (a) signs up for the newsletter, (b) books a 15-min founder call, or (c) self-serves 10 free credits — without ever seeing corporate fluff or generic "AI is changing procurement" filler.

**Content hub = content marketing engine, not a blog.** Every asset lives inside a three-node graph:

```
Blog post ←→ Email(s) ←→ LinkedIn post(s)
```

A post that does not have ≥3 newsletter references and ≥2 LinkedIn amplifications is either (a) not finished or (b) should not have been written.

## 2. Positioning

### Competitor content diagnostic

| Competitor | Content shape | What we do differently |
|---|---|---|
| **procure.ai** | Thought-leadership essays, VC-polished tone, light on specifics | We publish **operator-level tactical detail** — field-level examples (e.g., SAP S/4HANA BP object fields, VIES pitfalls, IATF 16949 tier verification steps) |
| **Coupa** | Enterprise marketing speak, "Business Spend Management" category bloat, gated whitepapers | We are **ungated by default** (except 3–4 deliberate lead magnets), direct, and never use "holistic" or "business spend management" |
| **Jaggaer** | Source-to-pay suite copy, category-strategy essays targeting CPOs at $1B+ firms | We target **mid-market procurement operators ($50M–$500M spend)** who ship campaigns themselves, not direct-to-CPO Gartner-ranking content |
| **SAP Ariba content** | Product marketing + analyst co-authored pieces, emphasis on GNP-scale networks | We emphasise **time compression (30h → 1h) and multilingual discovery** — the two places incumbents do not compete |

### Procurea's content wedge (the three things we own)

1. **The 30-Hour Problem** — time cost of manual sourcing, broken down mechanically. Our anchor. Every TOFU article ladders back here.
2. **Multilingual discovery** — 26-language supplier search. No incumbent markets this. Real moat — build the keyword cluster around it.
3. **AI sourcing pipeline demystified** — *what actually runs* when you click "Run Campaign." Incumbents gesture at AI. We publish the funnel (Google queries → URLs → screened → verified → contacted), the math behind each stage, and the scorecards we use. This earns trust from P1 buyers whose default posture is skepticism.

### Non-goals (what we deliberately will *not* publish)

- Generic "trends in procurement 2026" listicles without primary data
- ERP integration content that over-promises (our ERP story is honest: **pilot + Merge.dev + SAP S/4HANA roadmap** — see `roadmap-next-stages.md`)
- Customer case studies with fabricated names (all cases are "composite from beta cohort" or anonymized until we ship real ones)
- AI-generated sludge — every post gets a human specificity-check (see feedback-loop protocol, Definition of Done)

## 4. Brand Voice Principles

### 4.1 The five commandments

1. **Specificity over abstraction.** Never write "AI improves efficiency." Write "AI reduces a 30-hour manual sourcing campaign to 47 minutes — here is the funnel."
2. **Numbers before claims.** Every strong statement carries a number: "40% of supplier master data goes stale per year," "$2.6M hidden cost," "28 hours saved per campaign," "120 verified suppliers per run." If we cannot source the number, we do not publish the claim.
3. **Operator voice, not consultant voice.** Written by someone who has sent an RFQ and re-keyed supplier data into SAP at 11pm on a Thursday. Not written by McKinsey.
4. **Founder-direct for conversion pieces.** BOFU emails and LinkedIn personal posts are signed "Rafał" and read like a human wrote them one-off, not like marketing staffed a calendar.
5. **Short sentences. Active voice. No Oxford-comma essays.** Mid-market buyers skim on mobile between Teams calls. Paragraphs ≤3 sentences. Subheads every 250 words.

### 4.2 Non-AI-feeling checklist (applied pre-publish)

- [ ] Zero occurrences of: *"leverage," "holistic," "business-critical," "streamline," "empower," "unlock," "in today's fast-paced landscape," "at the end of the day," "revolutionize," "game-changer."*
- [ ] At least one concrete number in the lede
- [ ] At least one real-world example (product category + country + context) within the first 300 words
- [ ] No em-dash stacks (max 2 em-dashes per post)
- [ ] No generic title patterns ("The Ultimate Guide to X" is banned; "X: A 10-Criteria Framework for Y" is fine because it commits to a specific structure)

### 4.3 Tone by funnel stage

| Stage | Tone | Example opener |
|---|---|---|
| **TOFU** | Teacher + operator | *"A buyer looking for Turkish textile suppliers has three choices, and two of them quietly fail for the same reason..."* |
| **MOFU** | Analyst + comparator | *"Twelve AI sourcing vendors will tell you they do multilingual search. Here is how to tell which three actually do."* |
| **BOFU** | Founder + peer | *"Three procurement leaders I talked to last month asked the same ERP question. Here is the answer I wish someone had given them."* |
| **Email N10 / P1-11** | Personal ask | *"15 minutes. No deck. Three questions. My calendar: [link]."* |

## 5. Anti-Patterns (what NOT to do)

1. **No generic "Top 10" listicles.** Our competitors already rank for "top 10 procurement tools." We rank for "12 questions to ask before picking an AI sourcing tool" because the framework has teeth.
2. **No customer name-dropping.** Until we ship 3 real case studies with signed testimonials, every case is "a $400M manufacturer in our beta cohort" or "Maria (name changed, role: Category Manager at a textile importer)." Honest disclosure > manufactured social proof.
3. **No content without a keyword + a CTA.** Every post has a primary keyword from `keyword-map.md` and a Primary CTA from Notion Blog Pipeline. No exceptions.
4. **No calendar drift.** Publish date in Notion is commitment, not aspiration. If a post slips, the whole cross-channel graph (email sends, LinkedIn tease) slips — which means we do not slip without a reason documented in Content Factory weekly sync notes.
5. **No ERP over-promising.** Our current ERP integration reality:
   - **Pilot**: Merge.dev coverage for NetSuite, QuickBooks Online, Dynamics BC (see `/Users/rafal/.claude/projects/-Users-rafal-Documents-procurea/memory/reference_merge_dev_mcp.md`)
   - **Roadmap (Q3/Q4 2026)**: SAP S/4HANA native adapter (Merge does not cover this)
   - **Enterprise Custom**: Dedicated per-client integration for $25k–$100k ARR contracts
   Every ERP post carries a honest status badge. We do not write "seamless SAP integration" when we have a pilot adapter.
6. **No translating EN → PL via machine without review.** EN is flagship. PL is a deliberate secondary market. Each PL post gets a human review pass against `personas.md` tone notes (PL procurement buyers use different verbs than US/UK — "wyszukiwanie dostawców" not "poszukiwanie partnerów biznesowych").
7. **No posting on LinkedIn without a linked email.** Every LinkedIn post (except reactive/industry news exceptions) is tied to an email via `Linked Email` in Notion. If it has no email parent, it has no newsletter amplification — which breaks the recycle system.
